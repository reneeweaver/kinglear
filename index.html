The production titled Sci-Fi Lear takes Shakespeare's classical text and pushes it into a multi-dimensional formant, with digital projections and puppers of light. Tirabassi looks at the piece through a "Film Eye", as she directs the actors to move through the digital projections which create a holographic Galactic-Like Effect. Dynamic movement, visuals and sound express the poetry of the play for a better understanding of the emotions behind Shakespeare's sometimes complicated text. This exploration of of a 400 year old masterpiece (with 200 years of controversy) has brought about a documentary called Sci-Finding Lear as a result of a "New-Tradition" type process of finding a more human King Lear play and a more truthful interpretation of the writing due to less bounderies of exploration.

Tirabassi remarks excitedly, "I have been living and working in my art gallery in Pilsen for many years and the timing was perfect for Igloo."
